Whole blood donors

The minimum age requirement for whole blood donors is 16 years old.

Donors who are 16 and 17 years of age may be accepted if consent to donate blood has been obtained from parent or guardian.

The maximum age for first-time whole blood donors is 60 years old (up to 1 day before their 61st birthday).

Repeat whole blood donors can continue donating until they are 65 years old (up to 1 day before their 66th birthday).

Repeat whole blood donors who are 66 years old and above who wish to continue donating may still be allowed to if they had donated at least once in the last three years, remain healthy and pass the medical evaluation

Apheresis donors

The minimum age for apheresis donation is 18 years old.

New or first-time platelet or plasmapheresis donors are allowed to donate until age 60 (up to 1 day before the 61st birthday). You must have donated whole blood at least once before and not have a history of complications or reactions during blood donation.

1st time double red cell apheresis donors are only allowed to donate up to 50 years old (1 day before the donor’s 51st birthday). Donors must have donated whole blood at least once before and must have no history of complications or reactions during blood donation.

You should not donate blood if you have Toxoplasmosis or Brucellosis. You should wait for 2 years after recovery.

Donors with a benign thyroid condition can donate provided that they are:

  • Asymptomatic
  • More than 6 months from treatment with radioactive iodine therapy
  • More than 2 years from stopping treatment with anti-thyroid tablets
  • More than 8 weeks since commencing thyroid replacement therapy (e.g. thyroxine)
  • If on stable maintenance thyroid replacement therapy (thyroxine) and there have been no dose changes in the last 4 weeks,

    Please wait if you are still under investigation for a thyroid condition.

    Donors with history of thyroid cancer are permanently deferred.

    For more information or if you need further clarification, please call 6213 0626 and speak to our medical staff.

    You can donate blood if you are taking brewed herbs such as ginseng and lingzhi.

    Donors who took traditional or herbal medicine processed into pills, capsules or herbal extracts within the last 3 days may be accepted only  for whole blood donation. Please inform our medical screening staff if the last dose was taken within the last 3 days as only the red cells will be used in your donation.

    Platelet and Plasma apheresis donors can only donate 3 days after the taking the last dose.

    For more information or if you need further clarification, please call 6213 0626 and speak to our medical staff.

    Visiting or travelling to different regions of the world may make you ineligible to donate blood for a period of time. This is because some infections caught abroad can be transmitted through blood transfusion.

    Some vaccines or immunisations may affect your eligibility to donate blood. If you have received any vaccine or immunisation in the last 12 months, inform our medical staff.

    As long as you are well at the time of donation, there is no waiting period or deferral if you received the following vaccines:

    • Anthrax
    • Cholera
    • Diphtheria
    • Hepatitis A
    • HPV (cervical cancer)
    • Influenza / Flu vaccine (by injection)
    • Meningococcal
    • Pertussis
    • Plague
    • Pneumococcal
    • Polio (by injection)
    • Rabies
    • Tetanus
    • Typhoid (by injection)

    Wait for at least 2 weeks if you received the Hepatitis B vaccine.

    Wait for at least 4 weeks if you received the following vaccines:

    • BCG
    • Chickenpox
    • Dengue
    • German measles (rubella)
    • Influenza / Flu vaccine (by intranasal spray)
    • Japanese encephalitis
    • Measles (rubeola)
    • Mumps
    • MMR (measles, mumps, rubella)
    • Polio (by oral)
    • Typhoid (by oral)
    • Shingles (varicella zoster)
    • Yellow fever

    Wait for at least 12 months if you received vaccines :

    • Hepatitis B immune globulin (post exposure)
    • human rabies immunoglobulin (a component of rabies post-exposure vaccination)
    • vaccines that are still on trial

    Please call HSA blood bank at 6213 0626 if you:  

    • Received the smallpox vaccine
    • Received other vaccines
    • Are unsure of the vaccine or cannot provide adequate information regarding the vaccine

    You must weigh at least 45 kg to donate blood. To be an apheresis donor (plasma or platelet donor), you must weigh more than 50 kg.

    If you have Zika virus disease, you need to wait for 6 months after recovery to donate.
